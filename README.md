#  Africa: Feeding The World, Starving At Home

## The Question
African nations export millions of dollars worth of cash crops 
every year — cocoa, coffee, cotton, tobacco. But while the world 
enjoys these products, are African citizens going hungry and dying 
from malnutrition?

## What This Project Investigates
The relationship between cash crop exports, hunger scores, GDP 
per capita, and malnutrition deaths across African nations.

## What The Data Found

**1. Ethiopia — the most alarming case**
Exports $13.8M in cash crops annually while averaging 26,324 
malnutrition deaths per year. GDP per person is just $570.

**2. Madagascar — poorest and hungriest**
Hunger score of 41/100 (classified as alarming) with a GDP of 
just $454 per person — the lowest in this analysis.

**3. Kenya — the exception that proves the rule**
Highest exports ($21.7M) but also highest GDP ($1,446) and 
lowest deaths. Export revenue CAN reduce hunger — when it 
actually reaches citizens.

**4. The Real Conclusion**
Cash crop exports don't directly cause hunger. The crisis is 
that export revenue never reaches ordinary citizens in low-GDP 
nations — leaving families unable to afford the food their 
own country sells abroad.
